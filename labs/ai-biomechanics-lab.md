## Short description

The AI Biomechanics Lab at the University of Florida studies human movement with a focus on osteoarthritis and related musculoskeletal conditions. The lab integrates biomechanics, wearable sensors, and machine learning to better understand movement patterns, symptom variability, long-term disease progression, and real-world function.

## OA / biomechanics focus areas

- Knee OA and, to a lesser extent, hip OA
- Biomechanics of gait and daily activities in OA
- Wearable sensors and real-world movement analysis
- Machine learning for biomechanical pattern discovery
- Longitudinal movement analysis
- Movement variability and symptom dynamics

## Equipment / methods

- Marker-based and markerless motion capture (Vicon, Theia3D)
- Force plates (AMTI)
- Instrumented treadmill (Bertec)
- Wearable inertial sensors (Axivity AX6 and others)
- Ecological momentary assessment (EMA)
- Statistical and machine learning methods
